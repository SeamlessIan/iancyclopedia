Absorbtion of water depends on the transport of sodium and glucose across the intestinal epithelium. The water follows the salt and sugar automatically (due to wanting equal concentration, and because the salt and sugar cannot escape the layer, they must be actively transported in)
This happens at a fixed rate. If you have too much salt/sugar OUTSIDE in the intestine, rather than behind the epitheilum (which is what happens in gatorade, unless you REALLY dilute it) then you will absorb LESS water, since the concentration equals out sooner.

The number of receptors in the average person is what determines the avg. absorption rate. The dose of sugar/salt in pedialite or ORS is designed to saturate those receptors but leave AS LITTLE as possible in the intestine, so that as much water as possible is transported INTO the body.

That is why this is more effective than IV fluids... it's absorbed faster than the drip rate into one vein, since your intestines have multiple veins and arteries to carry away the fluid entering quickly.
