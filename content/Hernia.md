A hernia occurs when part of my intestines bulge through holes and end up where they're not supposed to be. 

## Inguinal hernia
In January 2017, this happened inguinally, which means that my intestines were trying to cozy up to my left testicle. I had it examined in February, and scheduled surgery for 1:15 p.m. on March 31, 2017.

## Treatment
The procedure for an inguinal hernia is to conduct a laparoscopic surgery, in which they will poke some holes in my abdomen, cut through some various tissue, and then implant a synthetic mesh under my viscera to keep everything where it belongs.

[Here is a video of the surgery.](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=YwEuZ8wVVNI) Watching it prior to the operation did not make me feel better about it, but watching it several times desensitized me to the fear a bit.
### Pre-surgery
- No food or drink 10 hours before the operation (from 03:00 on, in my case)
- Only sips of water until 6 hours before the surgery, then NOTHING
- No lotions or creams
- Bring an adult with me, preferably a responsible one
- Wear loose, comfortable clothing

### Questions for surgeon
- Will you install the mesh to cover only the side that's currently affected, will you cover both?
  - Only the affected side
- Can I get a video of the surgery?
  - No, but we'll take before & after photos
- Do you use a pre-surgery checklist?
  - (Yes, I saw the checklist in a notebook while waiting in the changing room)
- Please for the love of God be careful in there
- (To the other guy in the corner): Who are you and what's your role?
  - A: A resident, and assisting with the surgery.
- You are not performing the operation, only the surgeon is.
- Is it true that you do the surgery while I'm in the Trendelenburg position?
  - No
- I prefer not to take acetaminophen
  - Ok
- Will you have to install a breathing tube?
  - Yes. We will paralyze your muscles rendering you incapable of breathing
- Does the peritoneum heal, or will it be held together with stitches from now on?
  - We will not have to cut through that.
- Will any metal be left in my body, or is everything plastic? What do I need to tell doctors in the future?
  - Only the plastic mesh and some stitches will be left in you.
- Is it better for me to try to remain sedentary for the next few days, or is it better for me to try to move around?
  - Better to move around.
- What type of general anesthesia will the anesthesiologist use?
  - Desflurane and Propofol

## Post-Surgery
- 2017-04-10 Called Kaiser Permanente. Described protruding belly button and discomfort in that area. They reluctantly scheduled me for a follow up appointment on Thursday. After asking a lot of questions and trying to assuage my concern.
- 2017-04-12: got to see Dr. Pham today. It is normal for your balls to hurt and be bruised (and not because the anesthesiologist thinks it's funny to punch you in the dick to prove you're really unconscious). It is also normal for your belly button to turn from an innie to an outie. That's because there's a bunch of fluid underneath it, which will go away in another week or two. I will look normal again, assuming I looked normal before.
