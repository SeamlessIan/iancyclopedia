## Colorado
### Ballot Initiatives
** Guiding principles **
- By default, I will vote NO on everything unless I have a compelling reason to vote yes.
- The constitution is for guiding principles, and the statutes are for practical interpretations and measures
#### Amendment T: NO
- Exception to slavery prohibition for criminals
- Community service is a reflection of the idea of restitutive justice. Repaying society for the damage caused by ones actions heals both the community and the perpetrator of the crime. According to the DOC, prison work programs are voluntary anyway, so this measure would eliminate opportunities for prisoners.
#### Amendment U: NO
- Property tax exemption for possessory interests of $6,000 or less
- Give no special tax break to an arbitrary group. (I would consider voting for a measure that exempts the tax on the first $6000 of possessory interest for ALL parties no matter the total size of their interest.) Also, this belongs in a statute, not the constitution.
#### Amendment 69: NO
- ColoradoCare, a new state healthcare system
- While most yes voters surely have good intentions on this bill, it will not have a net positive effect for Colorado. It places huge additional taxes on employers, incentivizing them to limit payroll and/or move out of state. It establishes massive new taxes on employees. It further distributes risk across all residents irrespective of the risks inherent in their lifestyles. It incentivizes those with expensive medical costs to leave their states and let Colorado residents shoulder their burden. This will massively increase the size of government, and this growth will only increase and never be reversed within any of our lifetimes. Finally, I do not believe that state-run healthcare should be a guiding principle of the state, thus it doesn't belong in the constitution.
#### Amendment 70: MAYBE
- Increase in minimum wage
- This one at least is written as a guiding principle that I could potentially support: that minimum wage is reflective of cost of living. However, I foresee this eliminating minimum-wage jobs immediately and in the future, ultimately resulting in higher unemployment and an increase in the number of residents reliant on the government, so I personally will probably vote NO.
#### Amendment 71: NO
- Distribution and supermajority requirements for initiatives
- Even though I'm voting NO on all amendments above this, I won't vote to prevent measures from reaching the ballot. This measure serves to remove power from the people and concentrate it further in the hands of a few. Let the people continue to move to self-govern through ballot initiatives.
#### Amendment 72: NO
- Increase tax on cigarettes by $1.75 per pack of 20.
- This belongs in statutes, not the constitution, and it represents tyranny of the majority. Of course smoking is harmful, but the solution isn't to bloat government and taxes.
- A constitutional guiding principle would be something like: "Every X years, special taxes will be calculated for all products to compensate for the economic externalities imposed on the state and it's residents."
#### Proposition 106: YES
- Make assisted death legal under some circumstances
- Everyone has the inherent right over his or her own life and the time and manner of ending it if they so desire. This is absolutely a guiding principle that should be enshrined in the constitution.
#### Proposition 107: NO
- Open presidential primary elections
- Let the established parties calcify and alienate the populace. When the establishment gets sufficiently out of touch with the people, the people will change the system one way or another. Plus, if some of us get sick of it and make our own party, I'd hate to have to let everyone vote in our primary.
#### Proposition 108: YES
- Unaffiliated electors voting in primaries
- This will increase each individual voter's ability to choose the candidate from ANY party who best represents them.
