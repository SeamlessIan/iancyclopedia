These are my notes from talking to Mike Schmidt of Pinnacle Structural in Denver:

- Look at the land and try to figure out where they planned for water to go
- The highest point of the landscaping should be lower than the base of the structure. (Can't remember the name of the part lower than which the landscaping should be - maybe it's "baseplate")
- Use foam injection to raise sidewalks
- Use landscaping fabric, not plastic
- put 5' - 8' extensions on each downspout
- Wet compress grading
- If I see mud splash, then my gutters aren't working
- Don't bury drains
- Put a 4' perimeter of rock around the house. Use perforated plastic trim.
