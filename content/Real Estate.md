## Checklist when looking at property
- Is the foundation turning into dust?
- Is the house level?
- Are there safety issues?
- Would a dragon be comfortable there?

## Plan
My plan is to purchase a new property this year, then at least once every 2 years for the next 10 years.

In 2017, I will purchase a new property for myself to live in, and begin to rent out the Glendale property.

Two and a half years after moving out of the Glendale property, I will sell it. Having lived in the Glendale property 2 of the previous 5 years prior to selling it, I will [quality for the maximum exclusion tax credit](https://www.irs.gov/publications/p523/ar02.html).

### Financing
I will keep enough cash on hand to cover a few months' mortgage and HOA at the Glendale property, but I plan to leverage most of my resources towards a new property with faith that I will be able to find a renter in a short period of time.

Jason Lewis also suggested to option of leveraging the Glendale property with a HELOC if I need the extra capital.

### Accounting
Jason recommended that I set up a separate account for real estate. Income and expenses for real estate can all be tracked through that account, which I could then keep separate from my other assets. I can see the advantage that it's nice to see cash flow for real estate on its own, but ultimately it's all my money anyway so I don't know if there is a provable advantage to doing it this way.

## LLC
I will be able to maximize my tax advantage by keeping the property in my own name, and the only advantage of an LLC is to limit my liability. I will continue to keep the property in my name for the time being.

## Property management
I will not use a property management company right away, but will re-evaluate that decision once I have some experience managing property and know what the cost to me is to do it myself vs to hire someone to do it.

## Vetting tenants
Jason Lewis recommends requiring applicants to add him on social media, where he can get a good sense of their lifestyle and habits. A background a credit check is a good idea too. Jason also recommended a service called Cozy(https://cozy.co/) to vet tenants and collect rent.

## Tax
Look into 1031 exchange and owner occupied capital gains credit, , which requires the owner to have lived in the residence at least 2 of the last 5 years.
