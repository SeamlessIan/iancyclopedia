## State Amendments
### Amendment V - Yes
Yes. Who am I, or anyone else, to say which adults can run for election, or which candidates we have the option to vote for? I've never met a 21 year-old who I'd vote for, but I'm open to the experience.
### Amendment W - Yes
Yes. The new ballot format is less repetitive.
### Amendment X - Yes
Yes. I'm not aware of any reason why we need our own definition of hemp.
### Amendments Y and Z - Yes
Yes. Actually, I want to see an algorithm draw the boundaries so we don't have interested humans involved whatsoever, but until then, this seems like a step in the right direction to stop whoever's in power from abusing that power as much.
### Amendment A - Yes
Yes. We don't have slavery now, we shouldn't have it, and if we approve it this time we won't have to see it on the ballot next time and we can all focus on more pressing issues.
### Amendment 73 - Yes, even though I hate it
Reasons I'm voting for this:
- Our schools are under-funded.
Reasons I hate it:
- It allows the majority of people to vote a tax increase for other people at no cost to themselves. I'm not advocating for a flat tax here, I just think that we should all pay more, or that there should  a cost to everyone in voting for a tax increase - even a nominal one. We're all in this together here, or so I like to think, and so I would like to see our laws written.
- There is no plan for how this money will be used to improve education, nor any way to measure it's effects that I've seen.
### Amendment 74 - No
This sounds like a great way to let everyone sue the government and make sure nothing ever gets done again.
### Amendment 75 - No
"Let's get money out of politics by putting more money in politics!" Besides that, it's a very rigid bill. Why the $1 Million cut off? Shouldn't this scale based on how much the top contributor puts in? It's badly written now and will be hopelessly outdated in no time.
### Proposition 109 - No
Let's not force the transportation department to borrow money. I'd sooner see us raise the money through taxes now instead of kicking the can down the road. (see below)
### Proposition 110 - Yes
Sure, I'll pay taxes for roads.
### Proposition 111 - No
- Who am I to say who should be allowed to lend or borrow money and under what terms?
- Lending is already subject to market forces, which I think are stronger than legislative forces in this case
- This is a great way to reduce people's access to capital
### Proposition 112 - No
Control over issues like this (and many issues besides) should be decided at the local level. Let cities, counties, etc. sort this out, rather than trying to control it from the state level.
## City and County of Denver Ballot Questions
### 2A - Yes
This is a city issue, for city parks, and we'll get matching funds from the state. It's a no-brainer for anyone who likes to go to the park.
### 2B - Yes
I'm going to defer to the city council on this, and they seem to think it's a good idea.
### 2C - Yes
I think the police department can handle their own hiring strategy without my interference.
### 2D - No
I have not seen a case for why we need to add 2 more employees to the payroll. I am open to the idea, but I'm not convinced now.
### 2E - Yes
This measure actually makes sense for campaign finance reform, taking private money out of politics instead of putting more in like Amendment 75.
### 300 - Yes
This measure reimburses tuition AFTER the classes have been completed successfully. It's a good use of money, with more accountability in place than most measures that increase taxes.
### 301 - No
Mental health and suicide prevention are important, but so are a lot of things that aren't on this ballot to be voted on specifically. This should be left to the legislature.
### 302 - Yes
Diet and nutrition are near and dear to my heart, and I will pay more to have kids eat good food while their brains develop. This is also being done by funding 3rd party organizations instead of direct government intervention.
### 7G - Yes
A small tax, whose funds will be used to prevent more expensive problems down the road. It's a good idea.

## State Offices
### Governor - Polis
Jared Polis. It's weird to have the Republican in the race arguing for control at higher levels of government, and for legislating on social issues. Plus, arming teachers in schools? It does not seem likely to me that more guns in schools will mean less shootings. (See the amendment below on campaign contributions for more of this type of bizarre logic.)
### Secretary of State
No strong position, since we have at least two good candidates in the race. Wayne Williams is already doing a good job, and Jena Griswold is an energetic candidate who I believe would also be good in this position. I don't believe it's necessary to vote party lines on some of these state races, especially when there are good candidates from both sides of the aisle.
### Treasurer - Neither - Weiser
I'm not crazy about either of the candidates and don't have a strong position here. Dave Young does not seem to have any plan for the PERA budget issues, besides that he'll find a way to work it out once he's elected.
### Attorney General
Phil Weiser. Just research how the Aurora shooter's trial was handled by his opponent for more on this.
### Regent of the Universite of Colorado - At Large - Montera
Ken Montera. His priorities for the University and approach to managing the cost of higher education are more in light with my own values.
### State Senator - District 32 - Rodriguez
Robert Rodriguez. I know Senator Rodriguez and know that he is actively involved in this community and easily accessible to his constituents.
### State Representative - District 5 - Valdez
Alex Valdez. I've worked with Alex personally for over 7 years at EcoMark Solar. He's a competent leader and a hard worker and he'll do great in this position.
### RTD Transportation District Director - District C - Rivera-Malpiede
Angie Rivera-Malpiede. I like having someone with a behavioral science background in this position, and she is also the candidate who mentioned creative solutions to the last-mile problem as a priority.
