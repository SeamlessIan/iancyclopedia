title: Work Marathon
---

Notes from Sebastian Marshall webinar on how to conduct a multi-day, focused work marathon

## Small
- Prepare food/food plan beforehand
- Make environment comfortable (clean, laundry, etc)
  - including even haircut, shave, other things to improve mental state
- clear urgent obligations - emails, bills, etc. - anything that would sap mental energy
- block eliminate distractions
  - block distracting websites (Facebook, Reddit, etc.), 
  - delete phone apps / block notifications
- set expectations with people who might expect to hear from you/get an immediate response, to let them know you'll be out of contact for a few days
- set a email vacation auto-response so that you don't have to check email for several days

## Medium
- Mentally commit to focused work. Commit to getting enough sleep, resisting distractions
- Beginning of day:
  - morning ritual
  - get a few easy wins
- have a fallback plan: if you need to take some leisure, what would you do? (read X book, exercise, etc. NOT surf the net, drink and do drugs, learn organic chemistry, etc.)

## When things go wrong
When things go wrong, you lose momentum, you lose motivation, etc:
- revisit core assumptions & reasons for doing what I'm doing. Realign with vision, purpose, and intended result
- figure out just the next step
- do something else on the project, that you know how to do and can actually work on
- ask "what's the minimum viable product I can work on" aka scope down
- ask "what would a smarter version of me do here?" or "what would XYZ successful person do here?"
- ask "if I had to finish this by tomorrow, what would I do?"
- take a nap (30 min or 2 hours)
- look to end the day strong

## Large
- focus on 1 thing, or a few related things in a single domain
- pick something actually meaningful and important to work on
- pace yourself - max out at 8-10 focused hours of work, and balance that with sleep and other recovery items. Push yourself, but don't sprint the first part of the marathon
- Set two targets: one "bare minimum" and one "optimal." Set the bare minimum target bar VERY low and achievable. Most people inflate both of these beyond what's reasonable.
- reflect periodically during breaks, start, end of day:
  - what is inefficient?
  - what is going well?
  - what is causing me issues?
  - how am I doing on time? am I making good progress towards my goal?
  - make note of any epiphanies, patterns, trends

### If you are hitting a wall...
Should you continue to try to push through, or take a break? Ask yourself, "will I take damage if I stop? Will I take damage if I continue?"
- keep pushing for 60-90 minutes to see if you can break through. Give yourself points for pushing, even if it's not successful
